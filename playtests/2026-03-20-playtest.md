## Session Narrative Summary

### Scene 1 — Aboard the *Bombard* (Wharf Approach)

The session opens on the deck of the *Bombard*, a former military spelljammer (essentially a giant floating cannon) that Captain Reginald has been converting for passenger and merchant use. A mysterious crate has appeared on deck, labeled **"Argo: Brawl"**, and the entire ship is now ringed in a **purple St. Argyle's fire** — an omen the crew refuses to ignore. Reginald's crew has fled.

The party investigates the crate. Using a crowbar, they open it to find it packed with cheaply printed chapbooks — pulp fiction in the style of the popular in-universe author **Custer N'eight** (the Argo adventure series). The cover of the books depicts the very scene on the ship they are standing on. Among the books is **"Argo: Castana"** — a brand-new, unreleased entry in the series.

A young gnome named **Davin Pickfoot** is on the wharf, waiting with a cart. He is a would-be wizard apprentice who aspires to be a Spelljammer pilot; he is also an obsessive fan of the Custer N'eight stories. He has seen the cover of *Argo: Castana* and immediately understands that this is a street-date-breaking copy. He trades a book with one of the party and agrees to guide them to **Stargo Publishing**, the publisher.

Reginald offers to pay them to remove the crate and get rid of the Argyle's fire. He can't sail without crew.

### Scene 2 — Stargo Publishing: Lobby (Combat 1)

Stargo Publishing is an office building in the middle city of the Rock of Brawl, catering to the Mages Guildhall district. The lobby is a reading lounge — comfortable furniture, eight patrons absorbed in chapbooks, and a bespectacled receptionist named **Linda** (spelled "with a Y") behind a desk with a sign-in sheet.

Yegor sits down and openly begins reading the early-release book. This causes a scene: an **Orog** named **Hargrove** (half-ogre, half-orc, stats: Berserker) attempts to grab the book. A brawl breaks out. The book fans act on a "thousand-yard stare" autopilot, foaming at the mouth and single-mindedly trying to seize the books.

### Scene 3 — Stargo Publishing: Conference Room / Jackson's Office

**Jackson Starblood**, an Astral Elf and co-owner of Stargo Publishing, meets the party. He confirms the purple St. Argyle's fire was an **arcane tracking mechanism** placed on pre-release copies to find anyone who broke the street date. He's eager to get the crate back. He is suspicious of the party's story that the crate just appeared on their ship — he keeps probing about someone named **"Trenton"** (his brother and the other co-owner), whom he suspects is behind the unauthorized release.

The party, not knowing Trenton, accidentally confirms they don't know him. Jackson appears to accept their innocence but remains cagey. He invites them into his personal office to arrange payment.

## Playtest Feedback & Issues

### Writing / Presentation Issues

1. **Box text needs significant editing.** The DM stated multiple times that the read-aloud box text is not polished and would disappoint a paying customer: *"If I bought this adventure myself, I would be very disappointed with the author."* This is the single most important pre-publication task.

2. **The language needs work generally.** Even outside box text, prose phrasing was flagged as needing a pass.

3. **P4's destination hint was missing from the text.** When delivering the session-end hook (P4 pointing toward the Kwakistana system), the DM said *"I should have put this in the text."* This narrative bridge needs to be written into the adventure explicitly.

4. **Unclear item in loot table.** The spell scroll listed in Jackson's loot was described by the DM as *"I think that's wrong"* — possibly "sleep storm" is a transcription artifact. Verify the intended scroll.

5. **Trenton/Jackson relationship.** Players were confused about whether Trenton and Jackson were brothers (both named Starblood). This relationship should be telegraphed earlier in the adventure — possibly a framed photo or nameplate visible when the party first arrives.

### Encounter Design Issues

7. **Combat 1 (Lobby) — Encounter too strong for tier-1 four-person party.** The DM removed three cultists from the published encounter count before running it: *"We are a weak party... remove three cultists."* At level 2 with five cultists + an Orog/Berserker, the party was already having cultists knock characters prone and steal books in the same round. Consider building in a tiered encounter table or a sidebar for small/tier-1 parties.

8. **Combat 2 (Neogi) — Hatchling poison hit hard at level 2.** Garsh went down twice in this fight. The DM commented *"I said don't worry, it's like... they buffed these more than I thought they did."* At level 2 (6–15 HP), a Neogi hatchling crit (7 bite + 4 poison on a failed save) is potentially lethal. The DM noted the Neogi hatchlings used to turn into slimes when knocked unconscious, but removed that mechanic because *"it made the combat complicated."* That was the right call, but the raw damage may still need tuning for low-level play.

9. **The Neogi flees on taking any damage.** This came as a surprise to the DM mid-fight — he had to pull out the adventure text to confirm. Consider adding a more visible callout box for this behavior so it doesn't get missed during a live game.

10. **Octavian ran out of spell slots entirely before the end of Combat 2.** An Artificer at level 2 has very limited resources. The short rest between combats helped but was not sufficient. This is likely a party-composition issue rather than an adventure issue, but worth noting for the encounter notes.

### 2024 Rules Conversion Notes

These observations reflect the live on-the-fly conversion from the 2014 version of the adventure. They are reference points for the written 2024 update, not necessarily problems with the original design.

11. **Charmed Yoy during Combat 2 was a highlight.** The charm effect worked exactly as intended — Yoy was clearly charmed (obvious to perceptive party members), tried to persuade the party to leave without money (DC 9 Persuasion), and mostly failed because the party rolled well on Insight. The RP tension was enjoyable and the mechanic felt fair.

12. **The Orog stat block is no longer in D&D Beyond** (replaced with Berserker in the 2024 update). The 2014 adventure referenced the Orog by name; the 2024 edit should either substitute Berserker explicitly or include a simple stat block inline so future DMs aren't hunting for it.

13. **Alert feat interaction.** One player had the Alert feat. The DM noted some uncertainty about how it interacted with the surprise/perception check for the Neogi ambush. The current ruling (perception check to avoid disadvantage on initiative rather than full surprise) seemed workable — worth adding a short adjudication note to the adventure.

### Positive Observations

- **The St. Argyle's fire as a tracking mechanic** landed perfectly. Players immediately engaged with the in-world logic of "the fire appeared when the crate arrived" and the reveal that it was a publisher DRM scheme was satisfying.
- **Davin Pickfoot** was an effective NPC — his fan enthusiasm felt natural and his arc (aspiring pilot → actual helmsman of the *Bombard*) provided a clean callback at session end.
- **P4's dialogue** was clearly a table favorite. The mathematical-metaphor speech pattern, time-loop awareness, and the image of a potion-bottle-with-legs generated strong engagement.
- **The clockwork amulet.** The command word being "Reginald" and the amulet muttering P4's speech patterns is a wonderful piece of item design.
- **The in-universe books predicting the adventure** (Argo: Brawl accurate except for the consumption) is a strong horror hook and players immediately engaged with the paranoia implications.
- **Davin being cast as helmsman.** A clean, satisfying payoff for a minor NPC.
