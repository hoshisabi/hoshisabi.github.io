# A Pretty Normal Day

*Archived from http://www.msu.edu/~chapmand/boredom.html (captured Feb 18, 2005 via Wayback Machine)*
*A short story.*

---

Ray walked into the store, nervous but convinced of his plan of action. He reached into the jacket and clutched the heaviness of the gun. He knew nothing about the gun but he knew that it would work if he had to use it. The man in the black coat had told him that it would.

Ray stood before the store and wiped his brow. The man in the store and Ray studied each other through the thick front window. The man nervously clutched his newspaper and turned to the sports page, just as you would expect "his type" to do. Ray looked around the store, it seemed safe enough. Nothing seemed that dangerous about doing it now.

"That's it! I'm going to go in there now. I am going to do it," he said to himself. People turned to stare at the strange heavy set man in the fedorah cap and trench coat that seemed to be talking to himself, but they turned away when they realized that his hand never left his coat pocket. Mothers hurried their little children away from the store, hoping that nothing would happen to them and that someone else would be the victim of this weirdo.

Ray pushed open the door ... a mite too hard. It went crashing into the wall, startling the man behind the counter and starting a small ruckus in the shop.

"Hello...," Ray nervously advanced. "I'd like to buy a ... pet," Ray announced to the man behind the counter.

"Uh. What sort of pet were you looking for, sir?" the man queried.

"Something that's not too dangerous," Ray feebly answered.

"Ohhh... Um, would you like to buy a hamster. They're real tame?" the man offered.

"Has anyone ever been killed by one?"

The man started to laugh at what he perceived as a joke but noticed that Ray didn't laugh along. The man cleared his throat to disguise the laugh, yet it seemed unconvincing. Nonetheless, Ray didn't seem to notice.

"Nope. Not a single hamster related death in these parts, mister. Though, you could buy one of these nice terrariums and then you'd be able to keep a whole big slew of them, nice and safely."

"Uh... just one, to be on the safe side. And I'll take one of those terrarium things, as well."

*Damn*, the man thought, *almost got this patsy to buy more.*

"Want anything else? Some food?"

"Nah. No, wait a minute. Do they eat steak? You know, New York Strip... or anything like that?"

"Nope. They only eat this hamster food, here," the man said as he proffered the most expensive brand of hamster food. Still not much of a profit on that.

"Oh. I'll take some of that then."

"Ok. Thank you for shopping at Pete's Pets. Hope to see you again sometime."

"Thanks," Ray said as he hastily left with his new pet.

The phone rang. Pete answered.

"Hi, honey. Sell anything today?" said the voice.

"Ayup. Sold a hamster and a terrarium."

"Anything unusual, other than the profit. The store get held up again?"

"Nope. A pretty normal day," he lied to his wife.
