# DM Prep Report — PandoDnD Session 2026-05-20
## Bitopia: The Doomometer Pub Crawl

---

## Session Summary

You led the party through a no-combat marvel of gnomish ingenuity: a floating Artificer Convention hosted on the Inventorium, a massive structure suspended between Bitopia's twin mountains by three balloon-dirigibles. Professor Bartlesby — a harried inventor with a doomometer that was nearly pegged at 0.9 dooms — drafted the group to prevent a catastrophic explosion before dawn. Moving methodically through each balloon and airship, the party befriended a clutch of escaped fire-breathing lizards (including calming a panicked one mid-air after Lambeth's owl caught it in its talons), convinced two self-aware robots named Adam 8 and Susie 2 that shutdown wasn't death, solved a cooperative pipe-laying puzzle on a deflating balloon under something like time pressure, and navigated the mildly radioactive lab of Dr. Rutherborg to retrieve the stabilizers he'd unwittingly scavenged from the sleeping golems. By the time dawn divided the plane's two gravity layers in a sheet of brilliant light, the doomometer had settled to a comfortable 0.1 and the gnomes were buying drinks.

## Narrative Threads

- **Adam 8 and Susie 2, on ice.** The two robots are currently shut down, their stabilizers returned after Rutherborg agreed to give them back. Perri persuaded them that shutdown is not death — a promise that now has weight. If the party returns to the Galvanic Ward or encounters Dr. Capaconda's crew again, reactivating the robots is an open obligation and a potential recurring NPC relationship.

- **Dr. Rutherborg's glowing rock program.** The Radiant Spear's lab assistants are visibly ill; Rutherborg himself glows with health from prolonged exposure. He's studying "the strange properties of glowing minerals" — essentially radiation research in a D&D wrapper. The party now carries a piece of that rock. The science is not safe.

- **The lizard program.** The apprentice gnome Dalrymple was caring for five fire-breathing lizards being "microdosed" to suppress their fire breath. They're too young to breathe fire — except they did. The DM noted that if there had been extra time, one would have spontaneously evolved into a young red dragon. The party now holds a potion of fire breathing. The potential for a full-grown fire lizard, and Dalrymple's unauthorized attachment to them, is a dangling thread.

- **The figurine that isn't quite done.** Professor Bartlesby explicitly said "most of the bugs are worked out" when handing over the figurine of wondrous power (the flying machine). He wanted it field-tested. Someone in this party is going to find the remaining bug at the worst possible time.

- **Lambeth's patron and the Great Old One's interest.** Lambeth was sent here by a patron who "thought this would be a good obstacle to grow him for some unknown future opportunity." Whatever that future opportunity is, the Inventorium — with its mad science, dimensional travel, and artificer lore — may have given Lambeth something his patron wanted him to see or know.

- **Alistair's construct carries a condemned soul.** Revealed during character intros: the construct contains the consciousness of someone who wronged Alistair unforgivably, and feels pain every time it's in combat. The construct was sent into a radiation maze this session without hesitation. That's not going to go unnoticed by that consciousness forever.

## Unresolved Tensions

- **The glowing green rock and the deferred Con save.** Someone is holding Rutherborg's radioactive mineral. You said "whoever holds it has to roll a Con save at the end of the adventure." That hasn't happened yet — or it was glossed over in the wrap-up. Clarify who took it and run the save retroactively. Failure could carry a mechanical consequence (exhaustion, hit point maximum reduction, or a narrative sickness) that bleeds into the next session.

- **The robots' shutdown was Perri's promise, not the whole party's.** Perri made the emotional argument — that shutdown is a medically induced coma, not death, and that the party would make sure "when they come back it'll still be them." The other characters weren't part of that pledge. If Adam 8 and Susie 2 wake up altered, corrupted, or never wake up at all, that falls on Perri specifically.

- **Alistair's soul-jar construct takes combat damage.** The construct was deliberately sent into a radioactive hazard. Alistair played this for laughs. But it means the consciousness inside it suffered. How much Alistair cares — or deliberately doesn't — about that is a character thread that will eventually demand addressing.

- **The party has a potion of fire breathing and knows lizards that shouldn't be breathing fire are.** Dalrymple's lizards aren't supposed to have active fire breath yet. The party now holds exactly the thing that could accelerate that. Nobody said they should or shouldn't use it on a lizard. That's a temptation that's now in someone's pack.

- **No one asked why the lizards escaped.** The party found them and returned them efficiently, but the question of how five domesticated fire-breathing lizards got loose across three different airships was never answered. Someone let them out, or the cages failed in a suspiciously timed way.

## Preparation Guidance

### Pacing Advice

Last session was low-combat, puzzle-and-social-forward, and fairly brisk — five locations in 90 minutes. The party finished satisfied and with loot but no dangling immediate threat. That gives you breathing room to open the next session with something slower: a downtime scene, a social hub encounter, or a character-driven moment before the next crisis arrives. Don't open with a bang; open with a meal or a conversation. The tension can build from there. The deferred Con save for the glowing rock is a clean cold-open hook — handle it at the table before anyone asks, as a "by the way, when you left Bitopia" callback.

### Likely Player Actions

- **Return to the Galvanic Ward** or otherwise follow up on Adam 8 and Susie 2. Perri especially will want to know if the shutdown held and whether the robots are okay.
- **Identify or sell loot.** The Modrin treatise (150g to the right buyer), the bronze faceplate (250g to an artist), and the rose-colored glasses all have no named buyer yet. Players will want to find them.
- **Use the wingsuit or figurine.** Someone is going to test the figurine of wondrous power immediately. The wingsuit has a 24-hour lifespan and needs Tinker's tools to reset — whoever took it will either use it this session or lose the window.
- **Ask about the glowing rock's properties.** If a Con save happens and someone takes a consequence, they'll investigate. Dr. Rutherborg is the obvious source of information, or a sage.
- **Investigate the Bit Book Club / Pando connection.** Niko mentioned specifically joining pub crawls to find information about "a mysterious mechanical creature named Pando" met at the Bit Book Club. The Artificer Convention might be the right environment to surface that.
- **Downtime spending.** 10 days is significant — players may want to do crafting, training, research, or carousing. Have at least one downtime complication ready.

## Ideas & Suggestions

**1. The Con Save Opens a Thread.** Whoever holds the glowing rock rolls their Con save cold-open next session. On a fail, they develop a mild bioluminescence — not crippling, just visible in darkness. It becomes a social complication (hard to be stealthy, attracts curious looks in Sigil) and a signal that Rutherborg's science has real consequences. Perri's fire resistance or Lambeth's high CON make them fun candidates to have either pass or fail.

**2. Adam 8 Writes a Letter.** Once reactivated, Adam 8 sends a handwritten (literally hand-printed by a clockwork arm) note to Perri specifically, addressed "To the one who argued for us." The letter is brief, halting, and unexpectedly moving — the robot trying to articulate gratitude in a language not built for that experience. It says nothing plot-relevant. It just lands. And it means Perri has a robot who owes them one.

**3. The Figurine Fails at the Worst Moment.** During downtime or early in the next session's adventure, someone activates the figurine of wondrous power. It works — and then, at an inconvenient altitude (approaching a building, crossing a canyon, over open water), the "remaining bug" manifests: a 1d6 round countdown before it returns to figurine form with no warning. The party has to improvise a landing. No damage unless they're very unlucky, but it's a memorable moment and a callback to Bartlesby's caveat. It doesn't break the item — it just means it needs tinkering before the next use. Alistair can fix it with his tools.

## DM Tools

### 1. The Escaped Kaiju
**Name:** Young Red Dragon (Formerly a Lizard)  
**Difficulty:** Hard (CR 10, but the encounter is mostly contained)  
**Type:** Combat  
**Trigger:** Use this if the party gets curious about what "spontaneous evolution" means, uses the fire breathing potion on one of the lizards, or if you want to call back the session's biggest dangling thread.  
**Description:** One of Dalrymple's remaining lizards (or a sixth one the party didn't know about) has accelerated past the microdosing and fully evolved. It bursts out of a storage area aboard the Galvanic Ward or in Sigil — not immediately hostile, but terrified and burning things. The challenge isn't killing it (Dalrymple would be devastated); it's calming it, containing it, and getting it back into a space that won't burn. Speak with Animals, Animal Handling DC 18, or a DC 15 Persuasion with appropriate flavor can de-escalate before combat becomes lethal. If it fights: AC 17, HP 178, Fire Breath (recharge 5-6), 3 attacks. Awards no XP if subdued peacefully; awards 5,900 XP if fought.

### 2. Rutherborg's Sick Assistant
**Name:** A Plea for Help  
**Difficulty:** Easy (Social/Exploration)  
**Type:** Social  
**Trigger:** Use this after the party returns to Sigil or if someone makes contact with the Radiant Spear crew again. Good cold-open if the session needs a human hook.  
**Description:** One of Rutherborg's lab assistants — Mira, a gnome with a faint green tint to her skin — tracks the party down in Sigil. She knows they were on the Inventorium and saw Rutherborg's lab. She's frightened: the sick assistants aren't getting better, Rutherborg won't stop the experiments, and she doesn't know who else to go to. She wants someone to confront him, get him to stop, or get the other assistants out. Rutherborg isn't malicious — a DC 14 Persuasion or Medicine (DC 16) to explain the danger gets him to pause the experiments. The challenge: his research is generating real, valuable results, and the Inventorium leadership knows it. Stopping him has institutional friction. The glowing rock the party carries is exhibit A.

### 3. The Modrin Scholar
**Name:** Finding a Buyer for the Treatise  
**Difficulty:** Easy (Social, light exploration)  
**Type:** Social  
**Trigger:** Use when the party tries to sell the Modrin mechanical treatise (150g value). Good downtime scene.  
**Description:** The Society of Sensation keeps records of sensory stone collectors who've acquired Modrin texts. The contact — a human artificer named Hennicott — is intensely interested and offers 150g immediately. But he also wants to know where the party got it: Modrin texts are vanishingly rare, and the Inventorium shouldn't have one. He'll pay a bonus (additional 100g) if the party can get him access to Rutherborg or Capaconda for a conversation. If the party asks questions, he reveals that Modrin is a language of a sect of artificers who studied matter-plane mechanics before the Spellplague — and that several texts from that tradition describe devices that can stabilize planar tears. DC 13 Insight to notice he wants more than the book. DC 15 Persuasion to get him to tell you what he's actually looking for.

### 4. The Pipe-Dream Gambit
**Name:** Someone Wants the Party's Downtime  
**Difficulty:** Easy–Medium (Social)  
**Type:** Social  
**Trigger:** Use during any downtime scene if a player says they're researching, training, or carousing. Good for keeping downtime from being a handwave.  
**Description:** A Harmonium guard (Sigil's city watch, pompous and by-the-book) approaches whoever is most visibly active during downtime. The party apparently disrupted three officially registered scientific teams' demonstration schedules during the pub crawl — the pipe maze was part of a timed safety certification test, and the gnomes can't re-certify until the "interference" is officially documented. The guard needs a statement from each party member. It's bureaucratic nonsense, not an actual crime, but failing to cooperate (DC 12 Persuasion or just giving a statement) results in a hold on all Sigil-registered downtime activities until cleared. DC 16 Persuasion or a bribe (25g) to make the guard lose interest entirely. The actual certification was a success — the gnomes are delighted — but the Harmonium didn't ask them.

### 5. Waffles Gets Fan Mail
**Name:** A Celestial Steed's Admirers  
**Difficulty:** Trivial (Social/Humor)  
**Type:** Social  
**Trigger:** Use whenever the session needs a light moment, particularly early in a session that's about to get heavy.  
**Description:** Perri's celestial steed Waffles apparently made an impression on a young gnome who witnessed him helping carry pipes during the balloon crisis. That gnome is Dalrymple. He has left a small carved wooden statue of a dog at whatever inn or hub the party is using as a base — small enough to be charming, detailed enough to be recognizable. No note. Just the statue. If Perri investigates, Dalrymple is easy to find (he's on the Galvanic Ward, still tending to his lizards). He's embarrassed and won't admit he made it — DC 10 Insight to see through that. He's looking for a reason to leave gnomish research and do something that matters. This is a potential recurring minor NPC seed: an earnest apprentice gnome with a gift for animals and a growing sense that he's in the wrong career.
