## The Mountain

The mountain is not the city. The Netherese tore the top off of it to build Irenthal — Arveth is the mountain's name; the shearing is its oldest wound. When the city UnBecame, the mountain was restored. It prefers this. It has been waiting for centuries for someone to help keep it this way.

**What Arveth knows and fears:** The five crystals are concentration anchors for the sustained spell holding Irenthal in the never-was state. If all five are shattered, the city snaps back into existence — and when it does, it needs its base. The top of Arveth gets sheared again. The mountain is acting from self-preservation. It is not hostile to the party; it is the closest thing to an ally they have. But its agenda is specific: the crystals must not all be broken. It has been watching Durok because Durok is the most likely vector for an unintended shattering. Dr. Medicine is its chosen guardian because the mountain needs someone in the party who will understand what the stakes are — before it is too late to matter.

It does not speak in words. It speaks in images — showing, not saying. When it showed Clod images of hands being gathered through the cracks in the stone, it was showing him the threat: the Netherese reconstituting themselves, hand by hand. Clod read "offered hands" because he thinks like a merchant. He was being shown a warning. He declined and left, and the mountain accepted the transfer of the guardian role back to Dr. Medicine without complaint. It is patient. It has been patient for centuries.

It is not hostile. It is the closest thing to an ally the party has. The Coldpeak's fear and avoidance have served it well — avoidance means no one is trying to return the city. But Durok is. The mountain has been watching Durok the entire time. **It knows where Durok is.** The path to finding Durok runs through learning to receive the mountain's images.

## The Netherese City

- [ACTIVE] **The City That Never Was**: A Netherese city that attempted to shift itself out of reality to survive the fall of Netheril. The ritual required five mages to give their right hands. Instead of surviving, they erased themselves — but imperfectly. They left seams: the ruins, the crystals, the staff, the Chamber. The city is trying to remember itself. Every mind that receives the staff's signal is a thread pulling it back into existence. **The party now knows the Netherese phrase for it: "the city that never was."** The actual name slips away each time Alina tries to read it directly. The mountain's name is Arveth. The city's name is Irenthal.
  - **[DISCOVERED Session 5] The Shaping House (the crèche):** A building at enough distance from the city center that it did not phase out with Irenthal. The people are gone — phased with the city — but the building remains. Top floors sheared off cleanly. Lower floors intact, warm, kept swept by an enchanted broom until Session 5. It was the crèche where noble children (second/third born, with magical potential) were shaped before they were old enough to study magic — prepared to give up their right hands when the time came. Caretaker Igenio kept his hand (staff, not a dedicant) and was left behind when the city phased. His ghost dissipated in Session 5. The party has the Enduring Spellbook (his journal). The hidey hole is intact. **Igenio's right hand is a visible tell the party has not yet noticed.**
- [ACTIVE] **The Five Crystals**: Each crystal anchors concentration on a sustained spell of enormous power — this is what Alina read from the academy entrance in Session 4. Breaking all five ends the concentration. What happens when the concentration breaks is unknown to the party. **What the DM knows:** each crystal that opens retroactively replaces a piece of the environment — not appearing, but always having been there. The nameless gnome spellcaster was the first replacement (Session 2, Crystal 1). Four seals remain. The party knows the crystals matter; they do not know the cost of breaking them. **The full consequence chain:** all five crystals shattered → sustained spell collapses → Irenthal snaps back into existence → the city needs a base → Arveth's top is sheared again. The crystals are not a mystery to solve. They are a lock that is currently doing its job.

The initiation fresco showed an apprentice cutting off her own. The five mages gave their hands as the price of the ritual. The hands are still in the pit. The mages are not entirely gone.

## The Left Behind Theme

The emotional spine of the Irenthal arc. Keep returning to it.

- **Igenio** — waited centuries in the Shaping House for children who were taken with the city. He was staff, not a dedicant. Left behind because he was not citizen enough to be claimed.
- **Savin** — half-orc, between tribes, between bloodlines, belonging fully to neither. Doesn't hear the mountain. The mountain doesn't push against him because he already straddles the seam.
- **The dedicants' children** — taken with the city before they were old enough to choose it. They are in the never-was, with no awareness of time passing.
- **The Shaping House** — still standing because it was far enough from the city center. Everything in it set down mid-use. The beds still made.
- **The mountain** — left intact when the city phased out, but carrying the memory of what it lost and the fear of losing it again.

The juxtaposition: *what is now* (Savin, the orcs, Arveth, the party) vs. *what never was* (Irenthal, the floating city, the children, the dedicants). What exists alongside what has been suspended between existing and not existing. The party keeps finding rooms that stopped mid-use.
