# Savin

**Race:** Half-orc
**Role:** Hunter, Coldpeak tribe
**Status:** Active ally
**Pronouns:** he/him

## Description

Hunter for the Coldpeak tribe. Half-orc — his father is a Reghedsman who came to live with the tribe and married Broken Tusk's sister. His mother died shortly after he was born. He has grown up moving between both families, comfortable in neither world entirely. Has a scar on his right hand that he deflects questions about quickly.

Kaarsk has never hidden his contempt for Savin — he represents everything Kaarsk resents: the peace with the Reghedsmen, the dilution of the tribe's bloodline, the softening of what it means to be Coldpeak. Broken Tusk has been quietly grooming Savin for leadership. Savin does not want it.

## Known History

Found Pasha wandering in the blizzard after she separated from the party and brought her to the tribe. Was astounded the party encountered Rimetalon and survived — vouched for them to Broken Tusk on that basis.

## The Mountain

Savin has gone close to the mountain three times in his life — closer than any other Coldpeak hunter has gone and returned from. He felt nothing. No pressure, no presence, no suggestion. He has tested this deliberately and does not know what to make of it.

Once, as a young man, he reached out and touched one of the standing stones. It was warm. Something briefly registered that it knew the shape of him. He pulled back. He has told no one.

His father — a Reghedsman — told him what the mountain was and what it wanted. Not the tribe's version of the warning. An older version, from people who had traveled these lands for generations and had their own name for the place where the seam runs thin.

The mountain does not push against Savin because he straddles the seam between the world where the city persisted and the world where it didn't. He is not entirely a foreign presence to it. It may be able to show him images more clearly than it can reach anyone else — he is already half inside its frame. He does not know this yet.

He is the interpreter the party doesn't know they have.

## River

Both outsiders inside their own groups. Savin has grown up Coldpeak without quite belonging. River has been far from his people for a long time. Neither will say this directly. They'll recognize it in each other without having to explain it.

The natural first real moment between them: Savin has a scar on his right hand he doesn't talk about. River was closest to Creepy, who was taken because of what was in his right hand. Neither knows the other's piece of it yet. When those two threads touch, Savin and River are the right ones in the room for that conversation.

Let Eric set the pace. Lean toward this connection if he doesn't give strong direction of his own.
