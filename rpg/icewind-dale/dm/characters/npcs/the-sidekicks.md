## Creepy

**Type:** Goblin expert
**Status:** MISSING — presumed dead (Session 2)
**Player attribution:** Eric's suggestion

A goblin expert sidekick. Had a name — Eric knew it, the DM never did. The party called him Creepy. Made it through the underground lake, the piercers, and the gazer negotiation. Was present when the party inserted the Ioun stones into the Netherese door — at which point his name appeared in blood on the door. His name. The one no one else knew. He ran. He made it outside. Frostclaw was waiting. Body not recovered.

**This is load-bearing.** The Netherese city didn't find him by chance. He was marked. Eric built a backstory the DM doesn't know — that backstory is the thread. Talk to Eric privately: *what is Creepy's name, and what is the worst thing in his past he has tried to forget?*

Whether the door predicted his death or caused it or simply knew is unresolved. The party hasn't discussed it openly. This is not finished.

## The Nameless Spellcaster

**Type:** Gnome spellcaster
**Status:** ERASED — Session 2
**Player attribution:** Dominic's suggestion (a reference to his previous character — neither Dominic nor the DM realized what they were doing)

He did not die. He was replaced.

When the first Netherese crystal activated — when the party opened the door — one slot in reality was traded. The gnome was the trade. He is not dead; he is elsewhere, in a reality that was never supposed to exist. His body was buried under rock, but his absence is something different. The rock is real. The forgetting is the tell.

The party will notice before they understand. His details are already getting harder to place. That's not grief. That's the seam closing.

He had a Potion of Comprehension in his pack. The party has it now. They don't think about where it came from.

**DM note:** Let this develop slowly. Don't explain it. Let a player ask "wait, what happened to the gnome?" and let the table realize they've stopped being sure of the answer.

## The Gnome

**Type:** Expert (shortbow, +4, 1d6+2; can use Help as bonus action)
**Status:** DEAD (Session 1)

The DM never remembered this NPC's name during the session. Killed during the long rest in the warm cave — torn limb from limb by the creature, remains arranged in an unusual geometric pattern. This was the first direct evidence that the creature acts with deliberate purpose.

## Jordan

Not a sidekick — the name on a letter found in a crate from the destroyed caravan. A mother wrote to Jordan warning about Icewind Dale and sending cookies, a pink hat, and mittens. Jordan was apparently already dead under the snow at the time. The letter, hat, mittens, and cookies were looted by the party.
