Not yet a recognized hunter, though he would dispute this characterization loudly if pressed.

He is the tribe's most reliable source of elaborate pranks — specifically the stinky kind. He has been developing his craft for years, usually with Brekk's quiet and entirely plausibly-deniable encouragement. He takes this seriously in the way children take everything seriously when they are good at it.

He looks up to Kaarsk the way the tribe's children look up to Kaarsk: with a mixture of awe and careful distance. He has watched the lead hunter come back from things that should have stopped him, and drawn the obvious conclusions about what it means to be that kind of hunter.

The party's arrival complicated his worldview somewhat. He has extended River a specific form of trust — a carved knucklebone token, slipped over without commentary — which in Dak's social economy means something.

His parents were among those injured when Rimetalon attacked the camp. The tribe's hunting capacity is down. Dak has been waiting his whole young life for his first real hunt, and the circumstances are arriving faster than anyone planned.
