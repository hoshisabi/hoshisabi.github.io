Not the mountain. The mountain is where her reach touches the world from inside the Dale — and from inside the Dale, there is no difference that matters.

The Coldpeak call the mountain what everyone calls it: *the mountain over there*, spoken with deliberate care, without naming it. You do not name a thing you fear. The party inherited that name without quite knowing they were inheriting anything.

She communicates through images: stone, depth, patience, a thing held shut. Clod serves as her Harbinger, translating her images into language; his translations are confident and imprecise in ways that are beginning to seem load-bearing. When she showed him hands being gathered through cracks in the stone, he read "offered hands" because he thinks like a merchant. He is not wrong in the way that is entirely wrong.

Clod held the guardianship briefly — Durok's dagger, the title given without ceremony, the commission treated exactly as given. He fulfilled it. He found the mountain was pleased with him, and offered gifts: hands, through the cracks in the stones. Many hands. All the same kind. He had no use for hands. He left. He returned the dagger with the finality of a transaction closed, and the commission fell back to the one who had set its terms.

Dr. Medicine does not quite know what he has set the terms of. That is the standing situation at the close of Session 11.

*He will return — and then nothing ever will have been.*

## Roleplaying notes

When her presence becomes sound — in the stone's resonance, in vision, in what Clod cannot quite translate — she speaks in long, slow tones, stretching the vowels as if time runs differently where she is and no one has told her to hurry. *Youuuuuu couuuld doooo thaaaat.* Not menacing. Older than menacing. The pace of something that does not experience urgency as a category.
