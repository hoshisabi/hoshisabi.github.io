# Session 2 — May 2, 2026

Rested and de-frostbitten, the party pushed deeper into the cave and discovered it wasn't a natural formation at all — a short tunnel opened into a grand 30-foot chamber of worked Netherese stone, complete with carved murals of floating cities and ancient gods, an ice-covered marble door, and the remnants of someone else's camp. The camp yielded a limestone-crusted backpack, sparkly blue pebbles, and a journal that turned out to be the key to a logic puzzle etched on the door in what looked like Draconic but was actually Netherese — the same script used in the schools of Congenio Ioun. The puzzle identified four Ioun stones needed to open the door. Finding them required crossing an underground lake (Alina went straight through), surviving a ceiling full of piercers who turned out to hit considerably harder than expected in the 2024 rules, and then successfully conning three baby beholders out of the stones using snake oil and a solemn promise that the stones would "magically reappear in the cavern in two days." The door opened. As the party inserted the stones in the correct order, Creepy — the goblin spellcaster sidekick who had survived this long without ever remembering his own name — watched his name appear in blood on the door, bolted for the exit, and was immediately snatched by the Owlbear. His body was not recovered. Through the door: a bone bridge over a pit of several hundred crawling severed right hands (all right hands, notably, and every mage in the frescoes above was also missing theirs). The party ran. One hand scratched Dr. Medicine on the way out. In the Chamber of the Slumbering Crystal, the Owlbear crashed through the ceiling before they could catch their breath, killing the nameless goblin spellcaster under a chunk of falling ice. The fight that followed was brutal — the Owlbear breathed a cone of frost that restrained most of the party — but Berg drank the Potion of Growth, surged, and drove home 16 piercing damage, which was apparently enough to convince 14 feet of Snowy Owlbear that these particular humans weren't worth the trouble. She fled through the wall, sealed the tunnel behind her, and exposed a passage to the surface where the frozen bodies of a group of archaeologists had been waiting. Emerging into the blizzard, the party found Pasha — alive, unhurt, and accompanied by a Goliath hunter named Sook whose tribe, the Thunlakalaga, was nearby and whose chieftain, Old Goat, was apparently very interested to hear about people who had encountered the beast and lived.
